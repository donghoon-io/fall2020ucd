POV

  • Actionable problem statement → Generative Ideation
  • Good POV → Ideate in a directed manner by creating HMW questions
  • POV captures design vision : discover and articulate the meaningful challenge

  • 3 elements of POV : user, need, insight

[User] needs to [User's need] because [surprising insight]

↓ ↓

Verb synthesized statement that you can leverage in designing a

solution (intrigue people and hold the tension in POV)

(reason for the need X)

Ex)

  1. "A teenage girl needs more nutritious food because vitamins are vital to good health"
  1. "A teenage girl with a bleak outlook needs to feel more socially accepted when eating healthy food, because in her hood a social risk is more dangerous than a health risk."

No.1 is statement of fact, little excitement, little direction to develop solutions.

No.2 is actionable, potentially generative, problem statement

Develop a list of statements using imperative phrasing that outlines essential guidelines to create successful design solutions.

↓

How Might We Questions

Why

HMW questions → short questions that launch brainstorms

POV / Design Principles ——→ HMW Questions → Seed for ideation

↓

broad enough that there are a wide range of solutions

narrow enough that the team has some helpful boundaries

→ HMW questions will vary with the project and how much progress the team has made in project work

How to generate

  1. Begin with POV or problem statement
  1. Break that larger challenge up in to smaller actionable pieces
  1. Look for aspects of the statement to complete the sentence : How might we....

Brainstorm the HMW questions before the solutions brainstorms

Brainstorming

Why

  • Great way to come up with a lot of ideas that you would not be able to generate by just sitting down with a pen and paper.
  • Intention → to leverage the collective thinking of the group by engaging with each other, listening and building on other ideas
  • creates a distinct segment of time → intentionally turn up the generative part of your brain and turn down the evaluative part
  • can be used → 1. to come up with design solution, 2. come up with ideas

How

  • "Brainstorm mode" - Be intentional about setting aside a period of time
  • Invest energy into a short period of time (15mins or 30mins) → high engagement
  • Get in front of a whiteboard or around a table, take an active posture of standing or sitting upright, Get close together
  • Write down clearly what you are brainstorming
  • Use HMW question

2 ways to capture the ideas of a brainstorming

  1. Scribe : legibly and visually captures on the board ideas that team members call out. capture EVERY idea, regardless of your own feelings about each idea
  1. All-in : Each person will write down each of his/her ideas as they come and verbally shre it with group. post-it notes

Facilitate a Brainstorm

why

Good facilitation → Generative brainstorm (coming up with many, wide-ranging ideas)

how (As facilitator, what should we do to facilitate a brainstorm?)

  • Energy : keep a pulse on the energy of the group. Slow down or get stuck → make an adjustment, Create a variation to the HMW statement to get the group thinking in another direction (prepare HMW options ahead of time) , have a few provocative ideas to re-energize the team
  • Constraints : Add constraints to HMW questions, create process constraints ex) time limit on each HMW statement
  • Space : plenty of vertical writing area → allows the group to generate a large number of potential solutions, balance between having a footprint that is big enough for everyone & not so large that some people started to feel removed / all members of the group should be able to reach the board in two steps. Each person has access to sticky notes&marker
